Getting Started

Before you begin, I highly recommend becoming familiar with The Five Fundamental Principles of NeverFap.

These principles will form the basis of everything you will learn, whether you decide to get started with the courses, read the articles or practice the mental health exercises.

Because in most cases, the reason why you're addicted to porn is because you've neglected at least one of these principles in some form.

These five principles are: Choice over mind, Balance over reward, Awareness over all, Struggle over none and Uncondition over judgement.

And until you master all five principles, it will be very difficult for you to truly be comfortable with your porn recovery and to succeed with any amount of certainty.

NeverFap Deluxe Practices

NeverFap Deluxe Practices focus solely on verifiable steps you can take on a daily basis to help you progress towards learning the skills to end your porn addiction.

This is where you'll be spending a majority of your time, practicing these skills and exercises so that you can become comfortable with using them in everyday situations.

The most fundamental practice you can learn is Observe Your Senses. It is the only practice referenced as part of the Introduction course and it is the exercise you will practice everyday, regardless of whatever other exercises you choose to adopt.

It is arguably the most important thing you can do for your mental health, and to be quite honest, you could probably overcome your porn addiction with this one practice alone.

Do yourself a favour and commit.

NeverFap Deluxe Courses

NeverFap Deluxe Courses combine the NeverFap Articles and Practices into single, linear progression-based pathways based upon The Five Fundamental Principles of NeverFap.

They are designed to help you focus on a particular area of understanding, so that you can master that concept and feel confident with your ability to address your porn addiction.

As mentioned earlier, I recommend getting started with the Introduction course and working your way down the list, revisiting practices you personally found useful.
